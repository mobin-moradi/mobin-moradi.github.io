## Research Interests

I am interested in utilizing condensed matter techniques to tackle problems in high energy physics. This particular viewpoint has been beneficial since the early development of particle theory and has led physicists to important ideas like Higgs mechanism, phase diagrams of gauge theories, conformal field theory, quantum strings and random surfaces, etc.

Here are two problems that have occupied my mind and might remain unsolved there forever.

 - Cosmological Constant Problem
 
 One can briefly summarize the problem as follows. Why does the cosmological constant determined by the IR scale and not the UV scale? There are cases in which the short-distance physics greatly suppresses the classical equations of motion. Quark confinement is a famous example. Many ideas developed to approach this problem, but the one that I am interested in is the self-destructive structure of de Sitter space. Unlike Minkowski space, de Sitter space seems to be unstable, just like a space filled with electric fields. Particles would generate at a constant rate, and the gravitational attraction between them suppresses cosmic acceleration.
 
 - Gauge/String Duality
 
It is an attempt to describe the flux lines of gauge fields with string theory. The correspondence is an isomorphism between single trace operators of gauge theory and vertex operators of string theory. The renowned AdS/CFT duality is one such example. I am interested in string theory versions of Yang-Mills theory, to better understand the quark confinement.
